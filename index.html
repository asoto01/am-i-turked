What does "Turk" mean?

Turk /tɜːrk/ noun

An overweight or obese individual. Derived from "turkey" — plump, carrying extra fluff, and often developing the signature neck wattle.

"Bro's been hitting the drive-thru hard. He's getting turked."

"I stepped on the scale after Thanksgiving... fully turked out."

Origin: The comparison to a turkey — a bird known for its round body, fluffy appearance, and distinctive neck wattle (the dangly bit). If you've ever seen a turkey strut, you get it.

But wait, BMI doesn't apply to me because...

Let me guess: you're "muscular" or "big-boned"?

Here's the thing: the BMI exception applies to roughly 1-2% of the population — elite athletes and bodybuilders. Yet somehow, everyone thinks they're the exception.

This is called self-serving bias. We accept information that flatters us and reject what doesn't.

BMI isn't perfect, but for most people, it's a reasonable starting point. If you're turked, you're probably turked.
